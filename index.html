Food for your thoughts.

Hearts are won through stomachs, so pack snacks and an open mind if you’ll be travelling this holiday season. Feed a stranger, hear their hopes, and learn from communities that aren't like yours.

America is disconnected.

More and more, people speak of a rift between two Americas. But we can’t engage with and heal a country we don't understand. For the coming holidays, many people will be travelling — let’s take advantage of this opportunity to talk with people outside our geographic bubbles.

So here's the plan.

  1. Take an hour out of your holiday.
  2. Grab some candy, muffins, or warm apple cider.
  3. Stand on Main Street, outside the supermarket, or wherever people are.
  4. Talk to a stranger, and offer something to eat in exchange for hearing what they’re hoping for in America right now.
  5. Listen and empathize.

Ready? Let's do it.

Commit to talk to strangers, and challenge your friends to do so too.

Be prepared.

  • Conversations could veer into uncomfortable territory. Listen, don't try to argue or escalate. Our goal is to understand feelings, not change them.
  • Protect yourself. Work in pairs, in a public place.

Share what you've heard.

Have a good conversation? Let the world know! Tweet or post on Instagram with #foodforyourthoughts, but be sure not to violate the privacy of anyone you've met.
